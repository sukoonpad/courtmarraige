Special Marriage Act 1954 — Kya Hai?

Special Marriage Act, 1954 ek Indian central law hai jo religion ke bina marriage register karne ki sahuliyat deta hai. Iska matlab hai — Hindu–Muslim, Hindu–Christian, Sikh–Muslim, kisi bhi combination mein couple legally marry kar sakte hain. Yeh Act specifically inter-caste aur inter-religion couples ke liye banaya gaya tha.

Is Act ke tahat marriage ka certificate Delhi Government officially issue karta hai. Yeh certificate India ke har court, bank, passport office, embassy mein valid hota hai. Visa application, bank loan, property registration — har jagah ye document accept hota hai. Detailed pricing yahan dekhein.

Court Marriage ke liye Eligibility Criteria

Special Marriage Act ke tahat court marriage karne ke liye:

  • Umar: Dulha minimum 21 saal, Dulhan minimum 18 saal ka hona chahiye.
  • Mental Capacity: Dono parties legally sound mind ke hone chahiye.
  • Unmarried / Divorced: Pehle se shaadi-shuda nahi hona chahiye. Agar divorce hua hai toh decree chahiye.
  • Blood Relation: Dono parties prohibited blood relation mein nahi hone chahiye.
  • Consent: Dono parties ki free willing consent hona chahiye.

Court Marriage vs Arya Samaj Marriage — Kya Farq Hai?

Bahut log confuse hote hain ki Arya Samaj marriage aur court marriage mein farq kya hai.

  • Arya Samaj Marriage (₹3,100): Hindu Marriage Act ke tahat. Sirf Hindu, Sikh, Jain, Buddhist couples ke liye. Hawan ceremony + certificate. Same day — no notice.
  • Court Marriage (₹6,100): Special Marriage Act ke tahat. Har religion ke liye valid. Government registrar ke saamne declaration. Same day with tatkal.

Agar aap Hindu couple ho aur fast + cheap marriage chahte ho toh Arya Samaj best hai. Agar inter-religion ho ya strongest legal certificate chahiye toh court marriage choose karein.
